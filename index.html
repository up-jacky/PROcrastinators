Background

The World Bank highlights that achieving universal access to safe water and sanitation in the Philippines is crucial for improving human capital, public health, and poverty reduction.However, current figures show that only a fraction of the population has access to these services, indicating significant gaps compared to regional averages. The government aims to meet UN Sustainable Development Goals by 2030, requiring substantial investments and increased support for local governments facing challenges such as limited funds and capacity. Climate change further complicates the situation, worsens existing strains on domestic water supply systems. To address these issues, a coordinated approach involving the national government, local authorities, and the private sector is essential to ensure sustainable and equitable access to water and sanitation services for all Filipinos.
